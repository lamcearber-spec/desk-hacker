*Saved: 2026-01-27*

## Core Philosophy

**"Confidently Boring"** - Focus on compound growth, not hype. The boring angle is differentiated because everyone else chases trends.

### 5 Core Insights
1. **Research Is Everything** - Cast wide net, gather deep context (30-60 min before creating)
2. **Human Taste Is The Differentiator** - AI generates options, you pick winners
3. **Speed Through Systems** - Right frameworks = zero to shipped in hours
4. **Boring Is Differentiated** - Everyone sells hype; compound growth wins
5. **Task-Based Agents Win** - Use specialized agents (SEO expert, conversion expert, strategy expert) for fresh perspectives

## The 5-Stage Build Sequence

### Stage 1: Research (Deep Context)
- Market research - competitors, gaps
- Competitor analysis - screenshot pages, analyze positioning
- Customer language - actual words they use
- Validation - are keywords searched? Is angle saturated?

### Stage 2: Foundation (Voice + Positioning)
- Brand voice extraction
- Positioning angles (3-5 options)
- Anti-positioning (what you're NOT)

### Stage 3: Structure (Keywords + Content Pillars)
- Keyword opportunities (gaps competitors miss)
- Content pillars (organize by themes)
- Quick wins (low-hanging fruit for 60-90 days)

### Stage 4: Assets (Landing Pages, Emails, Content)
- Landing page copy + design
- Email sequences (welcome, nurture, conversion)
- Lead magnets

### Stage 5: Iteration (Reject Until Right)
- "This is too generic. Make it more specific."
- Voice enforcement
- Quality gates - would you actually buy from this?

## The 8 Positioning Angle Types

1. **The Contrarian** - Challenge what everyone believes
2. **The Unique Mechanism** - Lead with HOW, not just WHAT
3. **The Transformation** - Before/after, the gap closed
4. **The Enemy** - Position against common villain
5. **The Speed/Ease** - Compress time or reduce effort
6. **The Specificity** - Hyper-specific about who and what
7. **The Social Proof** - Lead with evidence, not claims
8. **The Risk Reversal** - Make the guarantee the headline

## Expert Review Framework (Decision Making)

### The Problem with Single AI
- Same context window throughout
- No comparison or validation

### The Solution: Multiple Expert Agents
Spin up 3-5 specialized agents:
1. Agency Growth Expert
2. SEO Specialist
3. Conversion Expert
4. Industry-specific expert

**Where they agree = signal in noise**

### AI Ping Pong Technique (High Stakes)
```
Claude Opus → Create first version
    ↓
Gemini Pro → Critique it
    ↓
Claude Opus → Refine based on feedback
    ↓
GPT → Final validation
    ↓
Perplexity → Fact-check
```

## Use Case Strategies

### Info/Education (Courses, Coaching)
- Longer nurture (7-14 emails before pitch)
- Heavy on value, light on selling early
- Story-driven (your journey, student journeys)
- Positioning: Anti-Guru, Methodology Transfer, Community Angle

### Consulting/Agency
- Authority-first traffic
- Case studies as lead magnets
- Best clients come from best clients (referral compound)
- Positioning: The Specialist, The Methodology, The Anti-Agency

### E-commerce
- Creative-first traffic (product photos/videos)
- Every customer photo is an ad
- Email: Abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back
- Positioning: Quality Story, Founder Story, Lifestyle

### SaaS
- SEO + Product-Led
- "[Competitor] alternatives" keywords
- Trial onboarding → conversion emails
- Every integration = distribution channel
- Positioning: Simplicity, Speed, Anti-Enterprise

## Key Prompts for Our Agent

### Initial Research
```
We are creating a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] targeting [AUDIENCE].
What makes us different: [UNIQUE APPROACH]

Research:
- Market landscape & key competitors
- Customer pain points & positioning gaps
- Pricing models & service packaging
- Best practices to repackage for our target

Save findings as .md files.
```

### Positioning Angles
```
Use the Positioning Angles framework.

Context:
- Product: [WHAT YOU SELL]
- Transformation: [WHAT LIFE LOOKS LIKE AFTER]
- Competitors: [WHO THEY'D BUY FROM OTHERWISE]

Output: 3-5 angles with psychology and headline directions.
```

### Expert Review Checkpoint
```
Spin up task-based agents to review this:
1. Agency Growth Expert
2. SEO Specialist
3. Conversion Expert
4. [Industry-specific expert]

Each analyzes from their perspective.
Synthesize: Where do they agree? What needs work?
```

## Key Takeaways for Implementation

1. **Research first** - 30-60 min minimum before creating anything
2. **Save context as .md files** - Everything feeds into subsequent tasks
3. **Use methodology, not just prompts** - Frameworks > instructions
4. **Checkpoint with expert review** - After major outputs
5. **Iterate without shame** - First drafts are starting points
6. **Ship something** - Even if imperfect
