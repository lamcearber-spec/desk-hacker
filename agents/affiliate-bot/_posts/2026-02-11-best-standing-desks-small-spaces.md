## What Makes a Great Small Space Standing Desk?

### 1. Minimum Width: 36-42 Inches

Anything narrower than 36" gets cramped fast. You need room for:
- Monitor or laptop (20-24" wide)
- Keyboard and mouse (~18")
- A few inches of breathing room on each side

**Sweet spot:** 40-42" width handles a single monitor + laptop setup comfortably.

### 2. Depth: 24" Minimum, 30" Preferred

Standard desk depth is 30". For small spaces, 24" works if you're careful about monitor placement (use a monitor arm to reclaim space).

### 3. Vertical Space Efficiency

Some "compact" desks eat floor space with bulky legs or wide bases. Look for:
- T-frame legs (narrower footprint than C-frame)
- Low-profile bases
- Corner-friendly designs

## Small Space Standing Desk Setup Tips

### Mount Your Monitor

A monitor arm is **essential** for desks under 30" depth. It:
- Reclaims 6-8" of desk space
- Lets you position the screen at proper eye level
- Makes standing/sitting transitions smoother

**Budget pick:** VIVO Single Monitor Arm (~$35)
**Premium:** Ergotron LX (~$180)

### Go Wireless

Cables eat visual space. A compact wireless keyboard + mouse combo keeps your ~24" of depth actually usable.

### Use Wall Space

Floating shelves above your desk add storage without consuming floor footprint. Pegboards work too.

### Consider a Desk Converter Instead

If even 36" is too much, a **standing desk converter** sits on your existing desk:
- **FlexiSpot M2B:** 35" wide, height adjusts separately (~$200)
- **VariDesk Pro Plus:** 36" wide, two-tier design (~$350)

Downside: Converters take up desk surface area. You're trading horizontal space for vertical adjustability.

## What NOT to Buy

❌ **Manual crank desks under $150** — The cranks are tedious, and cheap ones wobble

❌ **Desks narrower than 36"** — You'll hate it within a week

❌ **Glass-top standing desks** — Reflective, show every smudge, poor for mouse tracking

❌ "Desk risers" masquerading as sit-stand desks — Most don't actually lower to sitting height
