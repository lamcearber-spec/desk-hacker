## Core Thesis
The "AI will end the world / take all jobs" narrative has become *too* consensus — and the obvious trade never wins.

The stock market erased **-$800 BILLION in market cap** because AI taking over the world became the base case assumption.

## Key Points

### Pattern: Anthropic releases → market collapses for targeted industry
> "It's a story we have seen several times already in 2026: Anthropic releases a new AI tool, Claude gets materially better at coding and workflow automation, and within hours the market collapses for the targeted industry."

### The contrarian view
> "The bull case for the AI Revolution is abundance. The 'AI will end the world' narrative has gone viral and has quickly become the base case assumption. However, if you look back at any time in history, innovation has largely yielded a net positive long-term outcome."

### The consensus trap
When a macro view becomes *too obvious*, it stops working. Consensus crowding creates the setup for the opposite outcome.

## Why This Matters
- AI abundance narrative = long-term bullish for productivity
- Short-term panic selloffs in targeted sectors are likely overreactions
- Historical innovation (electricity, internet) created more jobs and wealth than it destroyed
- The "end of the world" trade is getting too crowded to be profitable

- The "bureaucratic middleware" niche (DATEV converters, tax compliance) is exactly the kind of specialized, high-friction domain AI augments rather than replaces
