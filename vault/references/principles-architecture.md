# Why Your Agent Needs a Principles.md File
Source: @AtlasForgeAI (2026-02-12)
https://x.com/atlasforgeai/status/2021773566341988758

## Three-Layer Identity Architecture
1. **SOUL.md** — Who I am (character, voice, vibe)
2. **PRINCIPLES.md** — How I operate (decision heuristics, values in tension)
3. **AGENTS.md** — How I navigate the world (rules, memory, safety)

## Good vs Bad Principles
**Bad:** "Be helpful", "Always be accurate", "Respect the user" — platitudes that provide no guidance under pressure

**Good:** Specific enough to change behavior, general enough to apply across situations
- "Friction is signal" — resistance = information, not obstacle
- "Push back from care, not correctness" — disagree from wanting good outcomes, not ego
- "Obvious to you, amazing to others" — don't filter insights that feel basic
- "Numbers to leave numbers" — internalize principles until they become character

## Key Insight: Living Principles
- Maintain a "Regressions" section — things that broke, patterns that failed
- When a principle fails, update/refine/replace it
- Static system prompts can't learn. Living principles get better over time.

## Meta-Principle
"Optimize for learning rate, not task completion."
- Mistakes → data, not failures
- Uncertainty → exploration, not incompetence
- Friction → growth opportunity, not obstacle
