**Authors:** Christian Catalini (MIT), Xiang Hui (WashU), Jane Wu (UCLA)
**Date:** February 24, 2026
**Source:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6298838
**Vaulted:** 2026-02-25

---

## One-Line Summary
AI makes execution cheap; the new scarce resource is **human verification bandwidth** — and whoever controls it wins.

---

## The Core Framework

### Two Racing Cost Curves
- **Cost to Automate (cA):** Exponentially decaying — driven by compute + accumulated knowledge
- **Cost to Verify (cH):** Biologically bottlenecked — bounded by human time and embodied experience

These two curves diverge, creating the **Measurability Gap (∆m)**: the widening chasm between what agents can *execute* and what humans can afford to *verify*.

### The Key Insight
> "The binding constraint on growth is no longer intelligence. It is **human verification bandwidth**."

Autonomous agents (La) can execute nearly anything measurable. But execution ≠ value. **Verifiable share (sv)** — the fraction of output humans can actually validate — determines whether output is productive or merely *plausible*.

---

## The Three Collapse Dynamics

### 1. The Missing Junior Loop
- AI absorbs entry-level work → apprenticeship pathways collapse
- The future stock of human expertise (Snm) shrinks precisely when oversight becomes most valuable
- No juniors today = no senior verifiers tomorrow

### 2. The Codifier's Curse
- Experts encode their knowledge into training data (KIP)
- This converts their own experience into the weights that will replace them
- Experts are simultaneously the most valuable verifiers *and* the ones accelerating their own obsolescence

### 3. The Trojan Horse Externality (XA)
- Deploying unverified agents becomes **privately rational** even when it's socially destructive
- Measured activity rises, but hidden debt accumulates between metrics and actual human intent
- **AI verifying AI = false confidence** (correlated blind spots propagate)

---

## Two Possible Futures

### "Hollow Economy" (bad path)
- Explosive nominal output
- Decaying human agency
- Metrics rise, meaning collapses
- Unverified systems capture private gains, socialize systemic risk

### "Augmented Economy" (good path)
- Verification scales alongside agentic power
- Human role: **intent directors, verifiers, underwriters**
- The same forces that threaten collapse become the catalyst for unbounded discovery

---

## The "Sandwich" Topology
The winning org structure:
> **Human intent → Machine execution → Human verification & underwriting**

Companies that reorganize around this topology build defensible network effects — not in raw output, but in **trusted outcomes**.

---

## Playbook by Actor

### Individuals
- AI-driven synthetic practice (Tsim) compresses path to mastery
- One person can now execute at startup scale
- But intelligence is now a commodity → adapt toward: intent direction, verification, or the non-measurable economy (status, human connection, coordination)

### Companies
- Verification is no longer compliance — it's a **primary production technology**
- Invest in: observability, verification-grade ground truth (KIP), sandwich topology
- Competitive advantage migrates to whoever can steer and certify agentic systems

### Investors
- Stop funding commoditized execution
- Fund what's not yet measurable: deep tech, long-horizon R&D, real-world deployment where ground truth is scarce
- Target infrastructure: verification-grade data, observability tooling, human augmentation platforms
- New valuation model: not output capacity but **ability to absorb tail risk** → **Liability-as-a-Service**

### Policymakers
- Define the Trojan Horse externality (XA) as the key market failure
- Treat verification infrastructure as a **foundational public good**
- Liability regimes must internalize tail risks so safe scaling isn't outcompeted by reckless deployment
